Speed Camera violations Go Down After One Year of 24/7 Operation

By Joe Caffrey
August 1st 2022 Staten Island’s 192 Speed Cameras Began to Issue Violation Around The Clock The 24/7 operation issues $50 Speeding tickets to any vehicle caught on camera traveling 10 miles per hour over the posted speed limit. By August 31 51,000 violations were issued. The 20 locations with the most violations total 28,011 violations.
Decried as a municipal money grab by residents and in editorials in the Staten Island Advance newspaper, the violations numbers from August 2023 are down by over 10,000 violations to 40,000. Ydanis Rodriguez, the DOT Commissioner, says this was the goal all along.
“So, all decisions that we make on the speed camera are about safety improvements, just safety.” Rodriquez said about the process of choosing where the speed cameras are placed. “I feel the message is clear. People that don't want to have a ticket should drive the speed limit, which is 25 miles per hour.”
